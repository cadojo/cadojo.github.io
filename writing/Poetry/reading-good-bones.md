_This place could be beautiful, right?_  
When I was younger,  
I spoke without salesmanship.  
Each pitch cracked under anticipation of  
what the world was. Earth warmed and I turned before  
_realtors_ and stagelit songbirds  
sold me on selling.

The open house led to a space  
with decorated podiums,  
loud squawks, and split foundations.  
_A real shithole_. As black birds flocked,  
one podium grew, and the murder got its   
fill. With great purpose and no  
cause, the people lept.

Some flew as _stones thrown_.  
Many more followed passively,  
in loose formations. Through thick fog  
and amber waves of grain, all stormed and coughed and  
screamed between bathroom breaks and threats  
of what could be: patriotism  
by capital punishment.  

Congressional pins dropped, and now  
my siblings are not buying.   
I have little time to refine my pitch:  
_This place aspires to great good._  
_We need not tear it down completely —_  
_patchwork and common cause can_  
_deliver what you deserve_.
