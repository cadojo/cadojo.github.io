This place could be beautiful, right? When I was younger, I spoke without salesmanship. Each pitch cracked under anticipation of what the world was. Earth warmed and I turned before realtors and stagelit songbirds sold me on selling.

The open house led to a space with decorated podiums, loud squawks, and split foundations. A real shithole. As black birds flocked, one podium grew, and the murder got its fill. With great purpose and no cause, the people lept.

Some flew as stones thrown. Many more followed passively, in loose formations. Through thick fog and amber waves of grain, all stormed and coughed and screamed between bathroom breaks and threats of what could be: patriotism by capital punishment.

Congressional pins dropped, and now my siblings are not buying. I have little time to refine my pitch: This place aspires to great good. We need not tear it down completely — patchwork and common cause can deliver what you deserve.
